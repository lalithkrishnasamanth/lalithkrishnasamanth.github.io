I'm a Ph.D. researcher in [Mechanical Science and Engineering](https://mechse.illinois.edu/)
at the **University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign**, working at the
[Materials Research Laboratory](https://mrl.illinois.edu/).

My research lives at the intersection of **electrochemistry**, **atomic-force microscopy**,
and **machine learning**. The central question I keep returning to is: *what does an
electrode surface actually look like when it is immersed in a liquid and under bias?*
Answering that question requires building new experimental tools, interpreting angstrom-scale
images, and increasingly, training neural networks to help with both.

A few things I've worked on recently:

- Mapping the **real-space charge density** at electrode–electrolyte interfaces using 3D-AFM
  ([ACS Nano, 2022](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acsnano.2c10819))
- Using **deep learning** to sharpen AFM topography beyond the tip-convolution limit
  ([Nano Letters, 2024](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/acs.nanolett.3c04712))
- Watching **nucleation events** reshape electrical double layers in real time
  ([PNAS, 2025](https://www.pnas.org/doi/abs/10.1073/pnas.2421635122))

This site is where I think out loud — about experiments, methods, papers, and occasionally
about what it means to do science at very small length scales.
