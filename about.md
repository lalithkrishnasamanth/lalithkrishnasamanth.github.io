## Who I am

I'm **Lalith Krishna Samanth** (Lalith is fine), a Ph.D. student in
[Mechanical Science and Engineering](https://mechse.illinois.edu/)
at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign.
I work in the [Materials Research Laboratory](https://mrl.illinois.edu/).

By training I am an **experimentalist** — I spend a lot of time in the lab
coaxing cantilevers to feel surfaces that are submerged in liquid, at
sub-nanometer resolution. But over the years the analysis side grew into its
own thing, and now a meaningful fraction of my time goes into
**computational work**: molecular dynamics, continuum modelling, and more
recently deep learning applied to microscopy images.

The common thread is the **electrode–electrolyte interface**: the thin zone
where a solid conductor meets a liquid, where charge accumulates, ions
rearrange, and reactions happen. Understanding this interface at the atomic
scale is relevant to batteries, catalysts, corrosion, and biosensors — but
honestly, I find it fascinating even independent of applications.

## Research snapshot

| Theme | What I actually do |
|---|---|
| **3D-AFM** | Measure force–distance curves in three spatial dimensions to resolve solvent and ion layering at solid–liquid interfaces |
| **Operando electrochemistry** | Apply electrochemical bias while imaging, so the interface is in a real working state |
| **Charge profiling** | Extract the charge-density profile from 3D-AFM data to connect to classic double-layer theory |
| **Deep learning for microscopy** | Train encoder–decoder networks to recover surface topography obscured by tip-convolution artifacts |
| **MD + experiment integration** | Use classical molecular dynamics as a structural reference to deconvolute experimental 3D-AFM signals |
